Our
Process

We turn legal data into clear answers through secure ingestion, intelligent analysis, and attorney-ready insights end to end.
Phase one
Case Intake
The journey starts with intelligent discovery. Publius organizes your legal data from the start, scanning documents, emails, and case materials to surface what matters most.

‍‍Early risks, key themes, and critical signals are surfaced immediately, giving attorneys clarity on what they have and where to focus from day one.
Phase two
Data Normalization
We turn scattered legal files into clean, searchable information so attorneys can find what they need in seconds instead of digging through folders. ​

With that foundation in place, Publius then performs deep analysis of the documents, timelines, communications, and potential risks, helping attorneys decide which cases to pursue and where to focus their time and resources.
Phase three
Early Case Assessment
and Case Analysis
Publius turns processed data into decision-ready insight by tying its findings to each case's goals, risk profile, and real-world constraints, so attorneys can quickly see which matters deserve deeper investment and move forward with confidence. ​

At every stage, attorneys can ask the Publius AI Assistant questions in plain language and get clear, sourced answers directly from their case materials. Publius then assembles the full story of the case, pulling out key people, dates, events, and themes to create an early case assessment–style overview that explains what happened, what matters most, what is missing, and what should happen next. Documents and issues are continuously ranked against your case repository and trusted reference sources, ensuring attention goes first to the highest-impact evidence.
Phase four
Document Review
and Courtroom Strategy
Publius ingests emails, PDFs, messages, and other case materials and consolidates them into structured, review-ready document sets, surfacing likely key, sensitive, or high-impact items so attorneys can prioritize their effort where it matters most. From there, it generates strategy-ready work product—including normalized case timelines, curated exhibit sets, and targeted witness preparation materials—so teams move from document review to case theory, motion practice, and trial preparation with greater speed, clarity, and confidence.
